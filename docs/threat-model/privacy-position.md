# DigiD privacy position

## Privacy stance

DigiD should not assume every communication requires full identity disclosure.

## Core privacy principles

### Minimize disclosure
Only reveal the amount of identity information needed for the context.

### Support pseudonymous identity
Not all trusted interactions must be real-name interactions.

### Separate verification from overexposure
A receiver may need confidence that a sender is trusted without needing the sender's full legal identity.

### Disclosure should be policy-driven
Different use cases can require different disclosure levels.

Examples:
- public social posting may allow pseudonymous verified identity
- employment workflows may require stronger disclosure
- enterprise agent actions may require explicit org delegation but not full internal system detail

### Receiver clarity matters
The receiver should understand both:
- what has been verified
- what has intentionally not been disclosed

## Product implication

Privacy is not a side feature. It is part of whether DigiD is socially and commercially viable.
